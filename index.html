About Me

Greetings! I am a third-year undergraduate studying physics and computer science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. When I'm not puzzling over physics problem sets or grinding through arduous coding assignment, you can find me studying ancient languages and the important texts thereof, particularly the religious ones.

I'm also an undergraduate researcher in the superconducting qubits lab at MIT since February 2025, supervised by Will Oliver. With Réouven Assouly, I work on optimizing the qubit thermalization by experimenting with various cabling and attenuation techniques, and modelling the system computationally in order provide theoretical explanations for our findings.
